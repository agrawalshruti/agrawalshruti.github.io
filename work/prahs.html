Prahs

Native Advertising for Prahs Energy Drink

client: T Brand Studio + designlab

my role: design and research

Background

Asthma affects a total of 25 million adults and children and is responsible for 14.2 million physician visits and 1.8 million ER visits each year, just in the United States. Growth in global air pollution means that these numbers will increase over time, especially in young children. Costs to treat and manage asthma are also correspondingly rising.

Concept

The need for alternative energy, solar energy in particular, is ever-present not just Brazil, but globally. While Brazil recently has taken solar power initiative to double their solar energy efforts by 2024, countries like India have paired up with energy companies like GE to invest more in solar energy resources.

It is estimated that by 2018, the Brazilian photovoltaic energy sector alone will create between 60,000 and 90,000 jobs. Millions of Brizalians are unemployed and non-renewable natural resources are rapidly depleting, including the Brazilian rainforests.

Using the 2016 Olympics as a platform, Prahs will emphasize how Brazil, known for biofuels production and hydroelectric power, currently accounting for 70 percent of the country’s electricity generation, is currently facing nationwide draughts coastal water pollution and electricity rationing.

Execution

Introduce a full bleed an animation of the stages of producing and consuming solar energy or the animation can be the entire scroll down - from solar panels (that need shade), to an inverter, and net meter.

Lead an exposé style editorial, with content focused on energy waste. Use a scroll down with animated infographics illustrating the quantitative, monetary, and qualitative bene ts or advantages of using solar energy as compared to other forms of energy.

Each comparative anyalysis is divided to into subsections, answering common questions about solar energy costs, global impact etc... margins can be used to give silly but true facts about solar enery.

Add a responsive and interactive global map showing countries that don’t/do use solar enery and the amount of each country (map responds to movement of cursor from the top to bottom of the page with countries being highlighted to show use).

Visual Inspiration

Moodboard · styletile · logo

Option 1 - Techy Ultramodern

Thin, crisp lines with geometric overlays and black backdrop project a strong technological emphasis and spacious aesthetic. Bright contrast gives a gamified, futuristic look.

Option 2 - RetroFuturism

Warm colorful gradience and paper-like texture, with clean geometric shapes highlights both a sunny mood and retrofuturistic ideal.

Option 3 - Photoreal gradience

Smooth black silhouettes against a backdrop with varying shades of sunlight higlights nature’s beauty and the beauty of the human form. Typography is either black, more likely white, and rounded, but crisp to maintain elegant user interface
