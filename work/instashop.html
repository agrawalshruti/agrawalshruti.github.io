Instashop

Fresh and easy grocery delivery to your doorstep

client: instashop + designlab

my role: user experience, user interface

Situation

Goodmarket is a grocery franchise originating in the United States. Their customer satisfaction ratings have remained relatively consistent over the last four years, however their market shares have been decreasing by 8% each year.

Through research, Goodmarket researchers have determined that this decline is mostly due to the global increase in online grocery ordering and delivery products. Because it’s quicker, convenient, and provides equally fresh food - customers prefer online shopping to that in store. Shopping online means they can shop from their desks, home or even on the go using the mobile app.

Good Market plans to release their pilot online grocery ordering program — Instashop — in New York and San Francisco, iterate based on what they learned and expand their products to other cities around the country.

Goals

Business Goals: Google.org has paired with Designlab to design a mobile companion app to make asthma easier and cheaper to manage. AMMA’s wearable technology, which works together with its mobile app, helps by measuring its wearer’s breathing patterns, blood pressure, steps and sleep pattern among other vital statistics.

User Goals: The AMMA band can even signal nearby hospitals and doctors when it detects symptoms requiring emergency attention. The AMMA mobile app, in turn, uses these statistics and alerts to build individualized asthma care plans for its user and prevent future asthmatic attacks.

The Process

Central questions

I began designing for AMMA’s mobile companion app by asking a few key questions —

Who are AMMA’s primary users?

What are they trying to accomplish with the app and what are their expectations?

What are the most important functions of the app to the user?

Who are the major competitors in the field and what are they doing effectively/ineffectively to help their users and how?

PART I - Exploring User Types

Personas and empathy maps allow us to engage with Instashop’s intended users in ways that are much more user-centric. While personas provide user background and personality traits, empathy maps are designed to tell the user’s emotional story and emphasize pains/gains he or she has, relevant to the product.

Card Sorting

Card sorting is a great way for us determine how the Instashop’s intended user organizes information relevant to the product. In the case of the Instashop app/website, card sorting specifically helped separate individual food categories, which are crucial to user navigation.
