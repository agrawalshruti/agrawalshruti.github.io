Instashop

Fresh and easy grocery delivery to your doorstep

client: instashop + designlab

my role: user experience, user interface

Background

Goodmarket is a grocery franchise originating in the United States. Their customer satisfaction ratings have remained relatively consistent over the last four years, however their market shares have been decreasing by 8% each year.

Through research, Goodmarket researchers have determined that this decline is mostly due to the global increase in online grocery ordering and delivery products. Because it’s quicker, convenient, and provides equally fresh food - customers prefer online shopping to that in store. Shopping online means they can shop from their desks, home or even on the go using the mobile app.

Good Market plans to release their pilot online grocery ordering program — Instashop — in New York and San Francisco, iterate based on what they learned and expand their products to other cities around the country.

Challenge

The challenge for Instashop’s web and mobile site was to have both business and user goals met.

Business Goals

expand customer base & market share

  • Focus on busy young professionals
  • Attract an additional user demographic (mostly younger)

design and release a pilot program in ny & sf

  • Develop a seamless and efficient grocery app
  • Begin with 100 products first
  • Expand pilot program to other cities eventually

improve customer experience & convenience

  • Generate more revenue & value for the whole company

User Goals

navigate app easily and efficiently

  • Seamless checkout
  • Secure payment and easy re-ordering
  • Customized product recommendations

quick delivery and quality products

  • Add from a large selection of items
  • Same day delivery

have a positive user experience

  • Groceries arrive fresh and clean
  • Overall experience is easier than in person

Card Sorting

Card sorting is a great way for us determine how the Instashop’s intended user organizes information relevant to the product. In the case of the Instashop app/website, card sorting specifically helped separate individual food categories, which are crucial to user navigation.

Site Map

Instashop’s site map helps developers determine how many individual product pages and pages in general must be coded for the live site. Of course, the site map can evolve depending on future user needs, content changes, and business goals.

Using Information Architecture principles makes it easier to start building a more refined wireframe. In the instance of Instashop’s multiple card sorting categories and site map content, IA principles streamline which parts of the wireframe we should focus on before moving on to prototyping and the kind of questions he or she should ask about the final prototypes. For example, is it easy for the user to find desired actions in the wireframe? Are functions, actions learnable? Elements in the user experience delightful? etc…
